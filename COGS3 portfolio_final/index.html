Economics Courses taken at UCSD

(As of December, 2022)

ECON 1: Principles of Microeconomics

This course is an introduction to the study of the economic system. It introduces the standard economic models used to examine how individuals and firms make decisions in perfectly competitive markets, and how these decisions affect supply and demand in output markets.

ECON 3: Principles of Macroeconomics

This is an introductory macroeconomics course. It covers: unemployment, inflation, business cycles, monetary and fiscal policy.

ECON 100 A-B-C: Microeconomics

This series of courses covers economic analysis of hosueholds, firms and the imperfect market structure.

ECON 120 A-B-C: Econometrics

This series of courses covers econometrics and shows students how to use STATA.

ECON 131: Economics of the Environment

This course covers environmental issues from an economic perspective. It relates the environment to economic growth.
